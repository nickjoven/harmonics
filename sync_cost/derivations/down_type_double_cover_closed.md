## Statement of the closed derivation

Let G = ⟨τ, σ⟩ be the group generated by the Klein flip and the
y-cycle shift on the 6-point lattice `L = Z_2 × Z_3`:

    τ(x, y) = (1 − x, −y mod 3)
    σ(x, y) = (x, y + 1 mod 3)

`L` is a single G-orbit; G acts as S_3 on L.

**Theorem (D1, closed).** The dimensions of the invariant function
subspaces are:

    dim F^⟨τ⟩  =  |L| / |⟨τ⟩|  =  q_2 · q_3 / q_2  =  q_3  =  3
    dim F^G    =  |G-orbits|    =  1

Linear interpolation between endpoints gives the Phase D formula:

    K²_count(w)  =  (1 − w) · q_3  +  w · 1
                 =  q_3 − (q_3 − 1) w

Verified by explicit enumeration in
`down_type_phase_d1_eigenvalue.py`.

**Proposition (D2, structural).** The commensurability weight
`w_3` saturates at 1 at the framework's operating coupling K*,
because q_3 = 3 is an inner Stern-Brocot denominator (depth 2
in the Farey tree), not a cascade boundary mode. Boundary modes
(q = 6 at F_5/F_6) partially lock; inner modes fully lock
whenever the cascade is active.

Numerical check in `down_type_phase_d2_cascade.py` confirms
`w(q=3, K*) / w(q=6, K*) ≈ 25` — the q = 3 tongue is 25× wider
than the q = 6 boundary tongue at K*, consistent with q = 3 being
firmly interior.

**Corollary.**

    a_1(down)² / a_1(lep)²  =  T²_count / K²_count(w_3 = 1)
                            =  (q_2 · q_3) / 1
                            =  q_2 · q_3
                            =  6

Observed (PDG 2024): `5.989 ± 0.271`, agreeing with the derived
value at `0.04 σ`.

## The derivation chain

1. **Surface assignment** (`item12_down_sign_flip.py`): Klein
   parity splits sectors into orientation-reversing (leptons,
   up-type — native surface K²) and orientation-preserving
   (down-type — requires orientable lift to T²).

2. **T² count** (Phase A, Phase C/monodromy): discrete lattice
   `Z_2 × Z_3` with `|L| = q_2 · q_3 = 6`. Unconstrained.

3. **K² count at w = 0** (Phase C/monodromy): Z_2 free quotient
   of L by Klein flip. For q_3 odd, no fixed points, count
   `= |L|/2 = 3`.

4. **K² count at w = 1** (Phase D, Phase D1): fully G-invariant
   subspace, dimension 1 because L is a single G-orbit. This
   replaces the earlier (heuristic) DoF(K²) = 1 claim with a
   rigorous orbit count.

5. **Interpolation** (Phase D, Phase D1): linear in w between
   the two endpoint dimensions, giving the 3 − 2w formula from
   the orbit-dimension gap.

6. **Saturation** (Phase D2): w_3 → 1 because q_3 is an inner
   cascade denominator. Structural claim; numerical check
   consistent.

## Up-type compatibility

The up-type factor `9/4 = (q_3 / q_2)²` comes from the **Fibonacci
shift** of the base pair `(3/2, 5/3) → (8/5, 3/2)` on the same
surface K² (Klein parity −1), not from a surface-count change.
The D1/D2 mechanism does not touch up-type.

The full per-sector squared-exponent pattern is therefore:

    a_1(lep)²   =  (q_2 / K*)²                         (saddle-node relaxation)
    a_1(up)²    =  (q_3 / q_2)² · a_1(lep)²            (Fibonacci shift on K²)
    a_1(down)²  =  q_2 · q_3   · a_1(lep)²            (T²-lift saturation)

with two orthogonal structural sources: Fibonacci shift within K²
(for up) and T²-lift saturation for down.

## Residual caveats

1. **D2 uses a structural argument, not a rigorous dynamical
   proof.** The framework's `boundary_weight.py` `HONEST SUMMARY`
   docstring notes that the rigorous coherence-window definition
   does not close under current machinery. The closure here
   inherits that caveat: if a future rigorous cascade model
   changes the inner/boundary split, the saturation argument
   would need revisiting.

2. **The linear interpolation is dimension-linear, not dynamics-
   linear.** D1 derives `K²_count(w) = 3 − 2w` as an interpolation
   of subspace dimensions between the two symmetry-invariance
   endpoints. A dynamically-weighted sampling (e.g. by tongue
   amplitude) might deviate; the observed value `w* = 0.999`
   sits so close to saturation that the linearity assumption is
   not tested in the physical regime.

3. **The S_3 symmetry group appears because q_2 = 2 and
   q_3 = 3 are coprime.** For general lattices `Z_{q_2} ×
   Z_{q_3}` the symmetry group changes, and the generalisation
   formula `R(w = 1) = q_2 · q_3` follows, but the `3 − 2w`
   coefficient is specific to the (2, 3) case. Other sectors
   with different `(q_a, q_b)` would need their own
   calculations.
