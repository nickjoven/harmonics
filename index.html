The situation

General relativity and quantum mechanics are each confirmed to high precision, but they are incompatible at small scales and high energies. Both frameworks also contain numerical inputs that the underlying theory does not compute. A partial list:

  • Spatial dimension $d = 3$ is set by hand in every calculation.
  • Born's rule $P = |\psi|^2$ is a postulate (1926). No derivation from deeper principles exists in standard quantum mechanics.
  • The Planck/Hubble ratio $\sim 10^{60}$ is parameterized, not computed.
  • Dark energy $\Omega_\Lambda = 0.685 \pm 0.007$. QFT's vacuum-energy estimate exceeds observation by $10^{121}$.
  • Spectral tilt $n_s \approx 0.965$ is set by the choice of inflaton potential — a free function.
  • Three generations; fermion mass hierarchy over 12 orders of magnitude. Generation count is an input; Yukawa couplings are fit to data.

The ingredients

Four mathematical objects, all undergraduate-level.

1. Integers. Distinguishable states that can be counted: $1, 2, 3, \ldots$ The minimum requirement for anything to be described.

2. The mediant. Given two fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$, form $(a{+}c)/(b{+}d)$. This is not a definition or a choice. When two oscillators at frequencies $\nu_1$ and $\nu_2$ couple, the resulting locked frequency lies between them (energy conservation) and has the smallest available denominator (because Arnold tongue width scales as $(K/2)^q$—larger denominators have exponentially narrower stability regions, so the system locks to the smallest $q$ first). The mediant is the unique operation satisfying both constraints (D29). Iterating it from $0/1$ and $1/0$ generates every positive rational number exactly once. The resulting tree is the Stern-Brocot tree (Stern 1858, Brocot 1861).

3. The fixed-point condition. Require that the output of a process equals its input. If a system distributes populations across frequencies, and the coupling depends on those populations, then self-consistency means: the populations you get out must be the populations you put in. This closes the equation on itself.

4. The parabola. Near a saddle-node bifurcation, the deviation from equilibrium scales as $\Delta\theta \propto \sqrt{\varepsilon}$, where $\varepsilon$ is the distance past the bifurcation threshold. This is universal—it is the local geometry of every Arnold tongue boundary in the circle map. It is not specific to physics. It is a fact about parabolas.

One further structure is derived, not assumed. Integers together with the fixed-point condition ($p \equiv 0$ identifying endpoints of the phase interval) force $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} = S^1$: the circle. The circle map, $\theta_{n+1} = \theta_n + \Omega - \frac{K}{2\pi}\sin(2\pi\theta_n)$, is the simplest invertible map on $S^1$ with a single coupling parameter $K$. Its mode-locked regions (Arnold tongues) and its winding-number staircase (the devil's staircase) are textbook dynamical systems (Arnold 1961).

The equation

Place a population $N(p/q)$ at every rational frequency $p/q$ on the Stern-Brocot tree. Write the self-consistency condition:

$$N(p/q) \;=\; N_{\text{total}} \;\times\; g(p/q) \;\times\; w\!\left(\frac{p}{q},\; K_0\, F[N]\right)$$

$g(p/q)$ is the fraction of oscillators near frequency $p/q$. $w(p/q, K)$ is the width of the Arnold tongue at $p/q$—how large a range of bare frequencies will lock to that ratio at coupling $K$. $F[N]$ is a functional of the entire population distribution. $K_0$ is the bare coupling.

In words: the number of oscillators locked to $p/q$ equals the total count, times how many have natural frequencies near $p/q$, times how wide the locking region is at the current collective coupling. The coupling depends on the population. The population depends on the coupling.

The distribution $g$ is not an input. At the fixed point, $g$ reproduces itself: $g^* = h(g^*)$. The equation determines its own frequency distribution. There are zero free parameters and zero free functions.

Two limits of one equation

The coupling parameter $K$ ranges from 0 (no coupling) to 1 (critical coupling, where the circle map becomes non-invertible). The field equation has two structurally distinct continuum limits.

At $K = 1$: Einstein's field equations. At critical coupling, the Arnold tongue widths scale as $w(p/q) \sim c/q^2$—the tongues fill the frequency axis. The Farey measure (weight $1/q^2$ per fraction) converges to Lebesgue measure on $[0,1]$. The sum over the Stern-Brocot tree becomes an integral. This integral is the Kuramoto self-consistency equation (Kuramoto 1984). Promoting it to a spatial manifold and computing the correlation tensor of locked oscillator phases produces the ADM evolution equations of general relativity (D13). The uniqueness is not a feature of the dictionary—it is a theorem. Lovelock (1971) proved that $G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu}$ is the only divergence-free, symmetric, rank-2 tensor in 4 dimensions built from the metric and its first two derivatives. The Kuramoto-to-ADM map satisfies all four Lovelock premises. No other field equation is possible at $K = 1$.

At $K < 1$: the Schrödinger equation. Below critical coupling, some oscillators remain unlocked—they drift through the Arnold tongue gaps. Linearizing the field equation around the partially locked state and applying the Madelung transform (writing $\psi = \sqrt{\rho}\,e^{iS/\hbar}$, a standard variable substitution that is not specific to quantum mechanics) produces the Schrödinger equation. The quantum pressure term is set by the Stern-Brocot osmotic velocity—the drift rate through the tree's gap structure (D12).

The Born rule. The exponent 2 in $|\psi|^2$ is not a separate postulate. At every Arnold tongue boundary, the phase deviation scales as $\Delta\theta \propto \sqrt{\varepsilon}$ (saddle-node universality). Squaring to get a probability is squaring the parabolic geometry. The Born rule is the population distribution at the fixed point—a property of the equation, not an additional assumption (D1).

Where $d = 3$, Lorentz symmetry, and the cosmological constants come from

Three dimensions. The mediant acts on pairs of integers. The matrices that implement it are $2 \times 2$ with integer entries and determinant $\pm 1$: the group $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$. Its continuum completion is $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$, which is characterized uniquely by four conditions the framework satisfies (arithmetic skeleton, projective action on ratios, Iwasawa dynamical trichotomy, Farey-hyperbolic geometry). The Bianchi classification of 3D Lie algebras eliminates every alternative (D15). Self-consistent adjacency—the requirement that geometry defines coupling and coupling defines geometry—forces the spatial manifold to be the group itself. $\dim\,\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R}) = 2^2 - 1 = 3$ (D14).

Lorentz symmetry. The order parameter of the Kuramoto equation is complex. Complexifying $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ gives $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C}) \cong \mathrm{Spin}(3,1)$—the double cover of the Lorentz group.

The (3,1) signature. The Klein bottle's phase states form a $2 \times 2$ matrix indexed by {locked, unlocked} $\times$ {locked, unlocked}. Three states are observable (at least one channel is locked). The fourth (both unlocked) is dark—coupling averages to zero. Three observable directions plus one dark direction gives signature $(3,1)$. The minus sign in $ds^2 = dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2 - c^2 dt^2$ is the dark state's negative contribution to the norm (D32).

Cosmological parameters from three integers. The fully closed non-orientable surface is the Klein bottle. An XOR parity constraint on the field equation collapses 1,764 candidate mode pairs to 4 survivors at denominators $q = 2$ and $q = 3$ (D19). These two integers, together with the Farey count at their product scale $|F_6| = 13$, determine:

  • $\Omega_\Lambda = |F_6| / (|F_6| + 6) = 13/19 = 0.6842$ —the dark energy fraction (D25). The Farey sequence $F_6$ counts the distinguishable rational states at the interaction scale $q_2 \times q_3 = 6$. There are 13. The partition between locked modes (dark energy) and unlocked modes (matter) is $13:6$.
  • $R = q_2 q_3 \times |F_6|^{q_2 q_3^3} = 6 \times 13^{54}$ —the Planck-to-Hubble ratio (D26). The exponent $54 = 2 \times 3^3 = q_2 \times q_3^d$ encodes the spatial dimension (D27).
  • $\Lambda\, l_P^2 = 13^{-108}/12$ —the cosmological constant in Planck units. The exponent $108 = 2 \times 54$ is twice the hierarchy exponent.

On the Klein bottle the vacuum-energy sum has four terms, not the torus's $\sim\!10^{183}$. The $10^{121}$ discrepancy of standard QFT is the ratio of those configuration spaces times the frequency scale ratio Planck/Hubble—counting, not fine-tuning. The numerical value is self-consistent but not independently predicted; see vacuum_energy.md.

Mass sector: integer conservation law

Each fermion sector walks the Stern-Brocot tree. The number of steps it takes before the walk closes is set by the integer conservation law

$$\text{depth} \times |3Q| \;=\; k_{\text{sector}}$$

where $Q$ is electric charge and $k_{\text{sector}}$ is the dimension of a dual gauge adjoint:

  • $k_{\text{lepton}} = (\dim\,\text{adj}\,\mathrm{SU}(2))^2 = q_3^2 = 9$
  • $k_{\text{quark}} = \dim\,\text{adj}\,\mathrm{SU}(3) = q_2^3 = 8$

The cross-link $q_2^2 - 1 = q_3$, $q_3^2 - 1 = q_2^3$ has the unique integer solution $(q_2, q_3) = (2, 3)$. The same two integers that set the Klein bottle's XOR mode filter are also the only pair whose adjoint dimensions satisfy the cross-link. The mass sector and gauge sector are two readings of one fixed point (mass_sector_closure.md, integer_conservation_law.py).

Consequences:

  • Generation exponent. $a_2 / a_1 = q_3/q_2 = 3/2$. The two-step formula predicts $\tau/\mu$ and $\mu/e$ to 0.07%.
  • Three generations; no fourth charged lepton. The Fibonacci backbone has exactly three base slots at depth $\leq 3$ (root, $3/2$, $5/3$). A fourth would require depth $\geq 4$, which violates $4 \times 3 > 9$. See fourth_generation_revisited.md.
  • Neutrino mass scale. With $|Q| = 0$ the integer law is trivial, so the neutrino depth is fixed by $\text{depth}_\nu = q_2^2 \times k_{\text{lepton}} = (q_2 q_3)^2 = 36$. The walk cycles back through $q_2^2 = 4$ weak interactions because there is no EM anchor. Result: three neutrinos at depths 35, 36, 37 with $m_\nu \sim 17$ meV, $m_3/m_1 = (K^\ast/2)^{-2} \approx 5.4$ (observed 5.77), $\Sigma m_\nu \sim 64$ meV (Planck bound $<120$ meV). Depth 36 = four antiperiodic Klein-bottle traversals, so the mode self-identifies: the neutrinos are Majorana. Testable by neutrinoless double beta decay (LEGEND-1000, nEXO, 10–20 meV sensitivity). See neutrino_mass_prediction.py.

Falsifiable predictions

  • $N_{\text{efolds}} = 61.3 \pm 0.7$, set by the eigenvalue separation of $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ (the golden ratio's minimal polynomial). Falsified outside $[59, 63]$. CMB-S4 ~2028.
  • No charged lepton beyond $\tau$, at any mass. Detection of a 4th generation charged lepton falsifies the integer conservation law.
  • Neutrinos are Majorana, with $\Sigma m_\nu \sim 64$ meV. Testable by neutrinoless double beta decay at LEGEND-1000 and nEXO (10–20 meV sensitivity).
  • Strong CP $\theta = 0$ exactly, from $\mathrm{Pin}^+(3)$ topology.
  • Dark-energy equation of state $w = -1$ plus a small twist-breathing correction. Testable by DESI / Euclid (~2028).

What is not yet derived

  • sin²θ_W running compatibility. The tree-scale rational $8/35$ matches the observed $0.2312$ at $M_Z$ to 1.1%, but does not run from that value to any high scale under SM 1-loop betas (sign and absolute value both fail; see sinW_running_check.py). Either the tree scale is not $M_{\text{Pl}}$ (breaking $R = 6 \times 13^{54}$ as the Planck/Hubble ratio), or the framework's "running" is the duty-cycle $K \to \mu$ mapping rather than SM RG. Open.
  • CKM mixing beyond the Cabibbo angle. $\theta_{12}$ is reproduced to ~3% by $\arctan\sqrt{m_d/m_s}$ (derived as a walk overlap); $\theta_{13}$ and $\theta_{23}$ need the full mass-matrix diagonalization, which has not been computed.
  • Cosmological dynamics $K(t)$. The Friedmann structure is identified; the time evolution of the coupling is not derived from field-equation iteration.
  • XOR filter in the continuum. The filter that selects 4 modes at $(q_2, q_3) = (2, 3)$ is defined on the discrete Stern-Brocot tree and does not survive the continuum limit via the frame bundle (D20, D21). The gauge group is recovered instead from the Klein bottle center $\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3$ via Cartan classification (D41–42); whether the two routes are equivalent is open.
  • Measurement outcome problem. Mechanism derived; specific outcome requires an inaccessible initial condition. Likely irreducible.
