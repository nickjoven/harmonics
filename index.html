One equation. Both sides of physics.

The claim

General relativity and quantum mechanics are the two pillars of modern physics. They use different mathematics, operate at different scales, and contradict each other where they overlap. Both are assumed, not derived.

This framework derives both from a single self-consistency equation. At full coupling ($K = 1$), its continuum limit is the Einstein field equations. Below full coupling ($K < 1$), the linearized limit is the Schrödinger equation. Same equation, two regimes, no free parameters.

It also computes, from three integers, the dark energy fraction, the ratio of the largest to smallest scales in the universe, and the cosmological constant—quantities that standard physics measures but cannot explain.

The equation

$$N(p/q) = N_{\text{total}} \;\cdot\; g(p/q) \;\cdot\; w\!\left(\frac{p}{q},\; K_0\, F[N]\right)$$

This is a fixed-point equation on the Stern-Brocot tree. $N(p/q)$ is the population at rational frequency $p/q$. $g(p/q)$ is the frequency distribution. $w(p/q, K)$ is the Arnold tongue width at coupling $K$. $F[N]$ is a functional of the entire population.

The equation says: the population at each frequency equals the total population, weighted by how common that frequency is, weighted by how easily it locks at the current coupling. The coupling itself depends on the population. The distribution $g$ is not given—it is determined at the fixed point: $g^* = h(g^*)$.

What it starts from

Four primitives. Each is derived or shown irreducible (D10, D29).

  1. Integers. Distinguishable states that can be counted.
  2. The mediant. Given $a/b$ and $c/d$, form $(a+c)/(b+d)$. This is the unique combining rule that preserves order, adds denominators, and converges stably. It generates the Stern-Brocot tree, which enumerates all rationals.
  3. The fixed-point equation. A self-consistency condition: the output must equal the input. This is what makes the equation above close on itself.
  4. The parabola. Near a saddle-node bifurcation, $\Delta\theta \propto \sqrt{\varepsilon}$. This is universal—it is the local geometry of every Arnold tongue boundary. The exponent 2 in $|\psi|^2$ comes from here.

From integers + fixed-point: $p \equiv 0$ in phase space forces $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} = S^1$. The circle is derived, not assumed. The circle map $\theta_{n+1} = \theta_n + \Omega - \frac{K}{2\pi}\sin(2\pi\theta_n)$ follows as the simplest map on $S^1$ with one coupling parameter.

Why these values and no others

The derivation chain has three links. Each step is forced: no free choices remain.

1. The mediant forces the symmetry group

Fractions $p/q$ have two integer components. The mediant acts by $2 \times 2$ integer matrices with determinant $\pm 1$. This is $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$. The continuum limit completes it to $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$. Four conditions—arithmetic skeleton, projective action on $\mathbb{P}^1$, Iwasawa decomposition (dynamical trichotomy), Farey-hyperbolic geometry—characterize $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ uniquely among all connected real Lie groups (D15). The Bianchi classification eliminates every 3D alternative.

Consequences: $\dim\,\mathrm{SL}(2) = 2^2 - 1 = 3$ spatial dimensions (D14). Complexification gives $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C}) \cong \mathrm{Spin}(3,1)$—Lorentz symmetry.

2. The fixed point at $K = 1$ forces Einstein

The field equation at full coupling ($K = 1$), in the continuum limit, produces the ADM evolution equations of general relativity. Lovelock's theorem (1971): the Einstein tensor $G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu}$ is the only divergence-free, symmetric, rank-2 tensor in 4D built from the metric and its first two derivatives. The field equation satisfies all four Lovelock premises. No other output is possible (D13).

3. The fixed point at $K < 1$ forces Schrödinger

Linearize the field equation below full coupling. The Madelung transform, with quantum pressure set by the Stern-Brocot osmotic velocity, gives the Schrödinger equation (D12). The Born rule ($|\psi|^2$) is the population distribution at the fixed point—parabolic geometry at every tongue boundary, not a postulate (D1).

Where the cosmological numbers come from

Three integers determine all cosmological parameters: $q_2 = 2$, $q_3 = 3$ (the Klein bottle's denominator classes), and $|F_6| = 13$ (the number of terms in the Farey sequence $F_6$).

  • $\Omega_\Lambda = |F_6|/(|F_6| + 6) = 13/19$ (D25–D28)
  • $R = q_2 q_3 \times |F_6|^{q_2 q_3^3} = 6 \times 13^{54}$ (D26, D27)
  • $\Lambda\, l_P^2 = |F_6|^{-2q_2 q_3^3} / (2q_2 q_3) = 13^{-108}/12$

The Klein bottle arises as the only fully closed non-orientable surface. An XOR parity constraint on the field equation collapses 1,764 candidate mode pairs to exactly 4 survivors at denominators $(q_1, q_2) \in \{(2,3), (3,2)\}$ (D19).

The logical chain

Two proof chains cover the full derivation.

  • Proof A (8 propositions): counting → mediant → Stern-Brocot → field equation → $d = 3$, $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ → ADM → Lovelock → Einstein.
  • Proof B (11 propositions): same start, diverges at $K < 1$. Unlocked oscillators → Madelung → Schrödinger. Parabola → $\sqrt{\varepsilon}$ scaling → Born rule.
  • Proof C (the bridge): $\Omega_\Lambda = 13/19$, $R = 6 \times 13^{54}$, $a_0$ from Klein bottle + Kuramoto critical coupling.

Open problems

The XOR filter that produces 4 modes at $(2,3)$ is a discrete structure. It does not survive the continuum limit (D20). The frame bundle route produces $\mathrm{Pin}^+(3) \cong \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathbb{Z}_2$ but not $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ and not Yang-Mills. Two paths remain open: the physical system is fundamentally discrete (the Stern-Brocot tree is the configuration space), or gauge structure emerges from the mean-field functional $F$, not the tangent bundle (D21).
