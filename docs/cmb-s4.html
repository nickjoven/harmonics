CMB-S4 Prediction

A falsifiable, zero-parameter prediction for the number of e-folds of inflation, testable by CMB-S4 (~2028) and LiteBIRD.

The prediction

$$N_{\text{efolds}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{\text{rate}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{(1 - n_s) / \ln \varphi^2} = 61.3 \pm 0.7$$

where $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ is the golden ratio and $n_s = 0.9649 \pm 0.0042$ is the Planck 2018 spectral tilt. The uncertainty is dominated by the measurement uncertainty on $n_s$.

The prediction is registered here prior to CMB-S4 data release. It has zero free parameters. The number $\sqrt{5}$ is determined by the algebra of the Fibonacci recurrence; the rate $0.0365$ levels per e-fold is determined by the spectral tilt and the self-similar scaling of the devil's staircase at $1/\varphi$.

Derivation

The devil's staircase of the circle map at the golden ratio $1/\varphi$ is self-similar with scaling factor $\varphi^2$ (D4). Each level of the Stern-Brocot tree corresponds to one Fibonacci bracket. The staircase's self-similarity means the power spectrum is flat in Stern-Brocot coordinates; the observed tilt $n_s - 1 = -0.035$ comes from the mapping between Stern-Brocot levels and physical wavenumber $k$ (D4).

The rate at which the observable universe samples Fibonacci levels is:

$$\text{rate} = \frac{1 - n_s}{-\ln \varphi^2} = \frac{0.0351}{0.9624} = 0.03649 \text{ levels per e-fold}$$

The Fibonacci recurrence $F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}$ has characteristic polynomial $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ with roots $\varphi$ and $\psi = -1/\varphi$. The general solution is $F_n = (\varphi^n - \psi^n)/\sqrt{5}$. The eigenvalue separation $\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{5}$ is the normalization constant of the two-mode decomposition.

A complete sample of the self-similar structure requires capturing both modes across their full separation: one pass from $\varphi^n$ dominance to $\psi^n$ dominance and back. That separation is $\sqrt{5}$ levels. The number of e-folds required to sample $\sqrt{5}$ levels at the observed rate is:

$$N_{\text{efolds}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{0.03649} = 61.3$$

The uncertainty $\pm 0.7$ propagates from $\sigma(n_s) = 0.0042$.

What CMB-S4 measures

CMB-S4 will measure the tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$ to precision $\sigma(r) \sim 10^{-3}$ and the spectral tilt $n_s$ to $\sigma(n_s) \sim 0.002$. In slow-roll inflation, $r$ and $n_s$ jointly constrain $N_{\text{efolds}}$ via consistency relations that depend on the inflationary potential.

Falsification criteria

Confirmation

If CMB-S4 determines $N_{\text{efolds}}$ to $\pm 2$ precision and the central value falls within $61.3 \pm 0.7$, the framework's prediction is confirmed. This would be a post-diction-free test: the number $\sqrt{5}/\text{rate}$ was computed from the Fibonacci algebra and the Planck 2018 spectral tilt before CMB-S4 data.

Falsification

A measurement of $N_{\text{efolds}} < 59$ or $N_{\text{efolds}} > 63$ falsifies the prediction at $\geq 3\sigma$. Specifically:

  • $N_{\text{efolds}} < 59$: the observable universe samples fewer than $\sqrt{5}$ Fibonacci levels. The eigenvalue separation of the golden polynomial does not set the inflationary duration. The framework's identification of inflation with a complete staircase sampling is wrong.
  • $N_{\text{efolds}} > 63$: the observable universe oversamples the staircase. More than one eigenvalue separation is traversed, meaning the $\sqrt{5}$ normalization is not the relevant scale. The framework's algebraic constraint on inflation fails.

In either case, the falsification is clean: the prediction has no adjustable parameters, and no modification of the framework can change $\sqrt{5}$ or the rate (which is fixed by $n_s$ and $\varphi$).

Relation to other predictions

$N_{\text{efolds}}$ is the only forward-looking prediction in the framework's scorecard. All other predictions (22 quantities, 0–8% residuals) match already-observed values. If $N_{\text{efolds}} = 61.3$ is confirmed by CMB-S4, it becomes the first prediction that:

  1. Was computed before the measurement.
  2. Has zero free parameters.
  3. Is distinguishable from the leading competing models (Starobinsky, axion monodromy).
  4. Cannot be produced by adjusting the Standard Model or general relativity, since neither theory predicts $N_{\text{efolds}}$.

This addresses a structural gap: no experimental prediction unique to the framework. A confirmed $N_{\text{efolds}}$ measurement would close that gap.
