Framework Walkthrough

A single-page technical summary of the derivation chain, what it produces, what it does not produce, and where the gaps are.

1. Starting point

The framework begins with one equation and four primitives.

The equation is the circle map:

$$\theta_{n+1} = \theta_n + \Omega - \frac{K}{2\pi}\sin(2\pi\theta_n)$$

The four irreducible primitives (D10) are:

  1. Integers $\mathbb{Z}$ — counting.
  2. Mediant $(a+c)/(b+d)$ — derived from energy conservation + stability (D29) as the unique combining operation on rationals satisfying monotonicity-preservation, denominator-additivity, and convergent-stability.
  3. Fixed-point equation $x = f(x)$ — self-reference.
  4. Parabola $x^2 + \mu = 0$ — bifurcation.

The circle $S^1$ is derived: integers + fixed-point equation force the phase space $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} = S^1$. The circle map is the dynamics on this space with coupling $K$ and natural frequency $\Omega$.

Free parameters: 0. Free functions: 0. Dimensionful inputs: 1 (a root oscillator frequency that sets physical units).

2. The field equation

The self-consistency condition on the Stern-Brocot tree (D11):

$$N(p/q) = N_{\text{total}} \times g(p/q) \times w(p/q,\; K_0 F[N])$$

$N(p/q)$ is the population of mode $p/q$. $g$ is the frequency distribution. $w$ is the Arnold tongue width. $F[N]$ is the order parameter, which depends on $N$ itself. The equation is self-referential: the population distribution determines the coupling, which determines the tongue widths, which determine the population distribution.

The fixed point $g^* = h(g^*)$ exists, is unique, and converges from arbitrary initial conditions (rfe engine). The equation has one parameter: the coupling strength $K$.

8. What keeps this from being a theory of everything

The following are the specific, identified gaps between this framework and a complete physical theory. They are listed in order from most to least fundamental.

8.1. One dimensionful input

The framework determines all dimensionless ratios and structural results. It does not determine one absolute scale. The electroweak VEV $v = 246$ GeV (or equivalently the root oscillator frequency, or $\hbar$ in SI units) is an input. No mathematical framework built from integers and rationals can produce a number with physical units. This is the same situation as general relativity (which does not determine $G$) and quantum mechanics (which does not determine $\hbar$). Whether this is a deficiency or a necessary feature of any mathematical description of physics is itself an open question.

8.2. The ADM dictionary [resolved by D46]

The mapping from Kuramoto variables to ADM variables is now derived (D46). Each entry is the unique identification consistent with the symmetries of the K=1 locked state: the metric is the unique positive-definite rank-2 tensor constructable from the phase field; the lapse is the unique dynamical scalar in [0,1]; the shift is the unique spatial vector from the mean phase; the frequency is forced by the Hamiltonian constraint coefficient; the coupling kernel is the unique self-consistent propagator. No free choices remain in the dictionary.

8.3. No derivation of fermion masses

The framework produces the gauge group, the charge table, the Higgs mechanism, and the coupling ratios. It does not produce the Yukawa couplings — the 9 quark and charged lepton masses, plus neutrino masses and mixing angles. These are the free parameters of the Standard Model that the Higgs mechanism leaves undetermined. The Klein bottle's 3-generation structure (D34, from Iwasawa KAN decomposition) gives the number of generations but not the mass hierarchy between them.

8.4. No quantum gravity

The $K = 1$ limit gives Einstein (classical gravity). The $K < 1$ limit gives Schrödinger (quantum mechanics). There is no derivation of the $K \approx 1$ regime where both effects are comparable — the Planck scale, where quantum gravitational effects are expected. The framework produces the two limits separately but does not produce a unified quantum-gravitational dynamics. The rational field equation on the Stern-Brocot tree is the candidate for such a description (it is exact at all $K$), but its physical predictions in the intermediate regime have not been computed.

8.5. Spatial coupling is assumed

The promotion from a single circle map to a spatially extended field theory (D12, Section 2) assumes diffusive nearest-neighbor coupling on a spatial lattice. This is physically standard but is not derived from the circle map alone. The spatial dimension $d = 3$ is derived (D14), but the form of spatial coupling (diffusive, local, isotropic) is an input.

8.6. The 1–4% gauge residuals are not fully closed

The duty cycle dictionary (D33) gives tree-scale predictions with 1–4% residuals attributed to the decoherence tax at $K < 1$. The running from tree scale to $M_Z$ matches 2-loop SM running to 0.3% RMS, but this matching uses the SM beta functions as the target. A fully closed derivation would compute the running from the framework itself (from the $K$-dependence of tongue widths) without reference to the SM. The scripts (gate_duty_predictions.py, decoherence_correction.py) do this numerically, but the analytic derivation of the beta functions from the tongue width formulas has not been completed.

8.7. Dark matter [resolved by D47]

The cosmic energy budget now partitions fully: $\Lambda : \text{DM} : b = 13 : 5 : 1 = 19$. The 6 matter modes at the product scale $q_2 q_3 = 6$ split into 1 irreducible mode (coprime to 6, baryonic) and 5 reducible modes (dark). $\Omega_{\text{DM}} = 5/19 = 0.2632$ (observed: 0.265, residual 0.7%). $\Omega_b = 1/19 = 0.0526$ (observed: 0.049, residual 6.7%). Dark matter in this framework is not a particle but the set of matter modes reducible to a single gauge sector — they gravitate universally but do not participate in the cross-sector (electromagnetic) interaction. The MOND transition at $a_0$ (D3, D8) is the scale where baryonic and dark sectors decouple, testable via $a_0(z) = cH(z)/(2\pi)$ with JWST-era kinematics.

8.8. No experimental prediction unique to the framework

Every prediction so far matches an already-observed quantity. The one forward-looking prediction — $N_{\text{efolds}} = 61.3 \pm 0.7$ — awaits CMB-S4 (~2028). The engineering targets (D22) propose benchtop experiments (N=3 Möbius resonator, Klein bottle memory), but these test the dynamical claims (mode-locking on non-orientable topologies), not the cosmological or particle physics predictions. A theory of everything requires at least one confirmed prediction that competing frameworks do not make.

Summary

The framework derives both known force laws (Einstein and Yang-Mills), the gauge group, the charge table, the Higgs mechanism, the full cosmic energy partition (13:5:1), 22 quantitative predictions at 0–8% residuals, and the resolution of four fine-tuning problems (cosmological constant, hierarchy, strong CP, dark matter ratio) — all from one equation with zero free parameters. What it lacks: fermion masses, quantum gravity, one dimensionful scale, a fully self-contained derivation of the gauge running, and an experimental prediction that would distinguish it from the Standard Model plus general relativity taken as given.
