Glossary

Key terms, objects, and concepts in the synchronization cost framework.

Mathematical objects

Arnold tongue
A region in the $(\Omega, K)$ parameter plane of the circle map where the rotation number locks to a rational $p/q$. Tongue width scales as $(K/2)^q$ near $K = 0$. The tongue boundaries are saddle-node bifurcations with universal parabolic geometry — the source of the Born rule exponent 2. See D01.
Born rule
The probability postulate $P = |\psi|^2$ of quantum mechanics. In this framework it is not a postulate: the exponent 2 comes from $\Delta\theta \propto \sqrt{\varepsilon}$ at every tongue boundary (saddle-node universality). The basin measure of a parabola is quadratic. See D01.
Circle map
The discrete dynamical system $\theta_{n+1} = \theta_n + \Omega - \frac{K}{2\pi}\sin(2\pi\theta_n)$. $\Omega$ is the bare frequency, $K$ is the coupling strength. At $K = 0$ it is a rigid rotation; at $K = 1$ the map becomes noninvertible and the devil’s staircase is complete. The foundation for all derivations.
Devil's staircase
The function $W(\Omega, K)$ mapping bare frequency to winding number in the circle map. A Cantor function: constant on every rational plateau (Arnold tongue), strictly increasing on a set of measure zero. At $\Omega = 1/\varphi$ and $K = 1$ it is exactly self-similar with scaling factor $\varphi^2$. This self-similarity produces the spectral tilt. See D04.
Farey sequence $F_n$
The set of irreducible fractions with denominator $\leq n$, ordered on $[0,1]$. $|F_6| = 13$ is the state count that sets $\Omega_\Lambda = 13/19$. See D25, D28.
Fixed point
A solution to $x = f(x)$. One of the four irreducible primitives. In the field equation, the fixed point is the self-consistent population distribution: the coupling $K$ depends on the population $N$ which depends on $K$. The universe is a fixed point of its own field equation.
Klein bottle
A closed non-orientable surface with no boundary. In the framework, the Klein bottle’s topology constrains the particle spectrum via an XOR parity selection rule at denominator classes $q = 2$ and $q = 3$. Its four mode slots map to the four known forces. See D19, D20.
Mediant
Given fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$, the mediant is $(a+c)/(b+d)$. This is not ordinary addition of fractions — it is the operation that generates the Stern-Brocot tree. Derived from energy conservation + stability as the unique way to combine two frequency ratios. See D29.
Möbius strip
A one-boundary non-orientable surface. In the framework, Kuramoto oscillators on the Möbius strip (antiperiodic boundary conditions) exhibit self-interference that forces rational phase divisions and selects odd modes. See D18.
Parabola $x^2 + \mu = 0$
The normal form of a saddle-node bifurcation. One of the four primitives. Universality of this normal form means the $\sqrt{\varepsilon}$ scaling at every tongue boundary is exact, giving $P = |\psi|^2$ with exponent exactly 2.
Rational field equation
The self-consistency condition $N(p/q) = N_{\text{total}} \times g(p/q) \times w(p/q, K_0 F[N])$ on the Stern-Brocot tree. Exact rational arithmetic, no continuum assumed. Its fixed point determines all coupling strengths and populations. See D11.
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$
The group of $2\times 2$ real matrices with determinant 1. Uniquely characterized by four conditions derived from the mediant: arithmetic skeleton, projective action, dynamical trichotomy, Farey-hyperbolic geometry. Its dimension ($2^2 - 1 = 3$) forces spatial dimension $d = 3$. See D14, D15.
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$
The group of $2\times 2$ integer matrices with determinant 1, generated by the mediant operation’s left and right moves $L = \binom{1\;1}{0\;1}$, $R = \binom{1\;0}{1\;1}$. The discrete skeleton that completes to $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ in the continuum limit.
Stern-Brocot tree
The complete binary tree of positive rationals, generated by the mediant. Every positive rational appears exactly once. This is the configuration space of the framework: mode-locked states live on the tree, and the field equation operates on it. Historically due to Stern (1858) and Brocot (1861).

Physical concepts

$a_0$ — MOND acceleration scale
The characteristic acceleration below which galaxy dynamics deviate from Newtonian gravity: $a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10}$ m/s$^2$. In the framework: $a_0 = cH_0 / (2\pi\sqrt{g^*})$ where $g^*$ is the self-consistent frequency distribution evaluated at $1/\varphi$. Predicted value: $1.25 \times 10^{-10}$ (4% residual). See D03, D09.
Collapse
Wavefunction collapse = dissipative convergence completing, with finite duration $\tau \propto 1/\sqrt{\varepsilon}$. Not instantaneous, not mysterious. The uncertainty relation $\tau \cdot \Delta\theta = \text{const}$ and the Zeno effect ($\varepsilon \to 0 \Rightarrow \tau \to \infty$) are corollaries.
Continuum limit
Two regimes: at $K = 1$ (critical coupling), the rational field equation reproduces the ADM formalism of general relativity, hence Einstein’s equations via Lovelock uniqueness. At $K < 1$ (subcritical), linearization of the unlocked population gives the Schrödinger equation. See D12, D13.
Cost functional
The central object. Physical dynamics are cost minimization on a synchronization landscape. Forces are cost gradients through different coupling channels: gravity (global mean field), electromagnetism (U(1) phase), strong (SU(3) confinement), weak (attractor transition).
Coupling $K$
The coupling strength in the circle map. $K = 0$: no interaction. $K = 1$: critical (noninvertible map, complete staircase, gravity). $K < 1$: subcritical (partial locking, quantum mechanics). $K$ is not a free parameter — it is determined self-consistently by the population at the field equation’s fixed point.
$d = 3$
Spatial dimensionality is forced, not assumed. The mediant generates $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$; the continuum limit completes to $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{R})$; self-consistent adjacency forces the spatial manifold to be the group itself. $\dim\,\mathrm{SL}(2) = 2^2 - 1 = 3$. See D14.
Fidelity bound
The self-referential constraint: the measurement instrument IS the measured dynamics. Unifies the MOND transition and wavefunction collapse as the same structure — a system resolving its own frequency against a reference it constitutes. The RAR interpolating function, collapse duration, uncertainty relation, and Zeno effect all follow. See D09.
$N_{\text{efolds}}$
The number of e-folds of cosmic inflation. Predicted: $\sqrt{5}\,/\,\text{rate} = 61.3 \pm 0.7$, set by the eigenvalue separation of $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ (the golden ratio’s minimal polynomial). Falsifiable by CMB-S4 (~2028).
$\Omega_\Lambda = 13/19$
The dark energy fraction. $13 = |F_6|$ (Farey count at depth 6). $19 = q_2 q_3 + |F_6| = 6 + 13$, where $q_2 = 2$ and $q_3 = 3$ are the Klein bottle’s denominator classes. Observed: $0.6847 \pm 0.0073$. Predicted: $0.6842$. Residual: $0.07\sigma$. See D25.
Spectral tilt $n_s$
The power-law index of the primordial power spectrum. The devil’s staircase at $1/\varphi$ is self-similar with scaling $\varphi^2$. The $k \leftrightarrow \Omega$ mapping gives 0.0365 Fibonacci levels per e-fold, producing $n_s \approx 0.965$ ($< 0.2\%$ from Planck 2018). See D04.
Stribeck friction
Velocity-dependent friction with a characteristic dip: static friction dominates at low velocity (stick), drops through a minimum, then rises (slip). The Stribeck lattice — a chain of oscillators coupled by this friction — is the numerical testbed. It demonstrates dual-regime operation: frequency conversion above bifurcation ($A > 0.8$), linear passthrough below.
Synchronization
Phase locking between oscillators. In the Kuramoto model: oscillator $i$ adjusts phase toward the mean field. The framework’s claim: all physical law is synchronization cost minimization. The circle map is the simplest discrete model of synchronization.

Notation

$p/q$
A rational winding number (mode-locked state on the Stern-Brocot tree).
$\Omega$
Bare (natural) frequency ratio of a driven oscillator.
$K$
Coupling strength in the circle map / Kuramoto model.
$w(p/q, K)$
Width of the Arnold tongue at mode $p/q$ and coupling $K$.
$g(\omega)$, $g^*$
Frequency distribution. $g^*$ is the self-consistent fixed point: $g^* = h(g^*)$.
$\varepsilon$
Distance from a tongue boundary (bifurcation parameter).
$\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$
The golden ratio. The most irrational number; sits at the widest gap in the devil’s staircase.
$F_n$, $|F_n|$
Farey sequence of order $n$; its cardinality.
