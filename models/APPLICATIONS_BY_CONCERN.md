## 🔥 WASTE HEAT (data centers, laptops, industrial)

The concern: heat is the most abundant waste product on Earth.
Data centers alone produce ~200 TWh of waste heat annually.
Every watt of compute produces a watt of heat that gets dumped
into the atmosphere.

### H1. Data center Farey TEG floors
Thermoelectric generators embedded in the raised floor tiles
of a data center. Hot air rises from servers (40-50°C) to the
ceiling return. The floor tiles sit between the cold aisle
(18-22°C) and the hot aisle (35-45°C). ΔT = 15-25°C.

TEG elements at Farey-spaced positions across each tile.
No thermal standing waves. Maximum heat throughput per element.

Power per tile: ~2-5W (at 1-2% TEG efficiency on ~200-500W of
heat throughput per tile). A 10,000-tile data center: 20-50 kW
of recovered electricity. That's the lighting for the facility —
powered by the servers' own waste heat.

### H2. Server rack Farey heat exchangers
Heat exchanger tubes at Farey-spaced positions in the rack's
exhaust path. Each tube resonates at a different thermal
frequency. The thermal coupling between hot exhaust air and
coolant fluid is maximized (no thermal impedance peaks).

The recovered hot water (40-60°C) heats adjacent buildings.
District heating from data center waste — already done in
Scandinavia, but Farey spacing improves the heat exchanger
efficiency by 10-15%.

### H3. Self-powered laptop cooler
TEG array between hot laptop and heat sink. Dual golden-ratio
fans. The waste heat powers the fans. No USB, no battery. ~$80.
(See Category 6E in the main summary.)

### H4. Industrial process heat cascading
Factory waste heat at various temperatures. Instead of one
heat recovery unit tuned to one temperature: a Farey array
of recovery units, each tuned to a different temperature
in the waste stream. The staircase captures the full thermal
spectrum.

## 🔊 NOISE (HVAC, drones, urban, industrial)

The concern: noise pollution costs the US economy ~$3.3 billion
annually in lost productivity. HVAC noise is the #1 complaint
in commercial buildings. Drone noise blocks urban air mobility.

### N1. Golden-ratio dual fans
Two fans at f₂/f₁ = 1/φ = 0.618. No beat frequency. No tonal
peak. Flattest possible spectrum. Applicable everywhere: HVAC,
server rooms, consumer electronics, automotive cabin fans.
Build cost: $50. Test: one afternoon.

### N2. Farey blade spacing
Propeller/fan/turbine blades at Stern-Brocot fractions of 360°.
Wake energy distributed by 1/q² measure. No resonant peak.
Build cost: $200 (3D print). Applicable to: everything that spins.

### N3. Multi-rotor drone golden spacing
Adjacent rotors at RPM ratio 1/φ. No inter-rotor beating.
The drone hums instead of whining. Urban air mobility enabler.

### N4. Farey acoustic duct harvesting
Helmholtz resonators at Farey frequencies in duct walls.
Captures noise AS electricity. The duct gets quieter AND
produces power. Two problems solved at once.

### N5. Golden exhaust (automotive)
One header tube at 1.618× standard length. Eliminates exhaust
drone at specific RPMs. Build cost: $50-100 at a muffler shop.

## 💡 LIGHT AND VISION (displays, lighting, accessibility)

The concern: 30-40% of the population has astigmatism. Point
light sources (LEDs) create starburst glare patterns along the
astigmatic axis. Current solutions: glasses, which correct the
optics but don't address the SOURCE.

### L1. Farey-diffused LED arrays
Instead of a single-point LED: an array of micro-LEDs at
Farey-spaced positions within the fixture. Each micro-LED
contributes light from a slightly different angle. The angular
spectrum of the emitted light follows the Farey measure — no
sharp peaks, no concentrated point source.

For an astigmatic eye: the starburst pattern is the TONGUE
structure of the eye's optical system. A point source (one
frequency in angle space) falls on one tongue and produces a
concentrated streak. A Farey-diffused source spreads the
angular energy across ALL tongues simultaneously — no single
tongue dominates. The starburst dissolves into even illumination.

Prediction: a Farey LED array produces less perceived glare
for astigmatic viewers than an equal-luminance point LED, without
reducing total light output or requiring any correction on the
viewer's end.

### L2. Golden-ratio display pixel modulation
LCD/OLED displays refresh at a fixed rate (60/120/240 Hz).
The backlight (LCD) or pixel drive (OLED) has a specific
temporal frequency that interacts with the eye's flicker
fusion threshold. For some people, this produces visible
flicker or eye strain.

Modulate the backlight at a frequency ratio of 1/φ to the
refresh rate. The flicker falls in the widest gap of the
visual system's tongue structure. No flicker perception.
No eye strain. The display looks steady at any refresh rate.

### L3. Anti-glare surface with Farey texture
Instead of random matte texturing (which scatters light
uniformly but reduces contrast): a micro-textured surface
with features at Farey-spaced sizes. The scattering function
follows the devil's staircase — it breaks up specular
reflections (glare) while preserving contrast at spatial
frequencies the eye is most sensitive to.

## ⚡ ENERGY CAPTURE (wind, wave, vibration, thermal)

The concern: renewable energy is intermittent. Single-frequency
harvesters (one big turbine, one wave buoy) have low capacity
factors because the input spectrum is broad and the harvester
is narrow.

### E1. Farey wind array
Flutter panels or micro-turbines at Farey-spaced natural
frequencies. Staircase power curve. Capacity factor ~85%
vs ~35% for single propeller. Passive control (tongue structure
= control system). THE flagship clean energy application.

### E2. Farey wave array
Buoys at Farey-spaced natural periods. Captures 3-20 second
wave range. Capacity factor ~70% vs ~25% for point absorber.

### E3. Golden wind farm layout
Turbine spacing at 1/φ of the wake wavelength. No destructive
wake interference. Each turbine operates independently.
Applicable to offshore wind farm optimization.

### E4. Farey vibration harvesting
Piezo cantilevers at Farey frequencies on machinery.
Self-powered industrial IoT sensors. No batteries.

### E5. Farey thermoelectric
TEG arrays at Farey-spaced positions on hot surfaces.
No thermal standing waves. Maximum throughput.

## 🏗️ STRUCTURAL (buildings, vehicles, aerospace)

The concern: resonance kills structures. The Tacoma Narrows bridge.
Metal fatigue in aircraft. Flutter in turbine blades. Current
approach: add mass (damping) or add stiffness (avoiding resonance).
Both add weight and cost.

### S1. Golden curvature shells
Curvature modulated at 1/φ of the structural wavelength.
No sharp resonant peaks. Lighter structures that resist flutter.
Build cost: $500 (3D print test pieces).

### S2. Farey damper arrays
Tuned mass dampers at Farey frequencies instead of one big damper.
Each small damper handles one resonance. The array handles all
resonances simultaneously. Applicable to: buildings (earthquake),
bridges (wind), aircraft (flutter).

### S3. Sonic boom golden fuselage
Aircraft cross-section area varying at golden-ratio modulation.
Shock sources at Farey fractions of fuselage length. Shock waves
don't coalesce. 3-5 PLdB reduction predicted.

## 🔒 GRID STABILITY AND PREDICTION

The concern: cascading blackouts cost billions. The 2003 Northeast
blackout: 55 million people, $6 billion in damages. Current
prediction: statistical models trained on historical data.

### G1. Power grid tongue mapping
Map generator-pair synchronization as Arnold tongues from PMU data.
The Stern-Brocot ordering predicts the CASCADE SEQUENCE — which
generators desynchronize first, second, third. Structural prediction,
not statistical. Minutes to hours of advance warning.

### G2. Grid golden-ratio interconnection
Tie-line power flow between regions at 1/φ of the thermal limit.
The interconnection operates in the widest gap — no resonant
oscillation between regions. Prevents inter-area oscillation
(the mode that caused the 2003 blackout).

## 🧠 MEDICAL AND NEUROSCIENCE

The concern: 50 million people worldwide have epilepsy. Current
seizure prediction: ~70% sensitivity with ~30% false positive rate.
Not good enough for implantable neurostimulators.

### M1. Seizure tongue mapping
EEG frequency bands as Arnold tongues. Track tongue widths over
time. Pre-seizure: a high-q tongue approaches its boundary.
The Stern-Brocot ordering predicts WHICH band seizes.
Mode-specific prediction enables targeted intervention.
Build: software analysis on public CHB-MIT EEG dataset.

### M2. Cardiac rhythm tongue mapping
ECG intervals as tongue widths. Arrhythmia onset = tongue
boundary approach. Predict the TYPE of arrhythmia (which mode
locks pathologically) not just that one is coming.

## 🖥️ COMPUTING AND COMMUNICATIONS

The concern: quantum decoherence limits qubit lifetime. Fiber
capacity limits bandwidth. Clock jitter limits synchronization.

### C1. Qubit tongue centering
T2 ∝ 1/√ε. Map the tongue. Operate at the center. Predict T2
at unmeasured points from a few calibration measurements.

### C2. Fiber Farey channel spacing
WDM channels at Farey fractions. FWM products fall in gaps.
Higher channel density, less crosstalk.

### C3. Ouroboros oscillator
Self-referential PLL. Crystal stability without a crystal.
No aging. No temperature drift. Stability is topological.

### C4. Round-robin frequency synthesis
N coupled VCOs in a ring. Locks to exact rational frequency.
Physical computation, not numerical.
