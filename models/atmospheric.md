# Atmospheric Stick-Slip: Lightning and Tornadoes

## The atmosphere lives at the tongue boundary

Severe weather environments hover at K ≈ K_c for the relevant
coupling parameters. The dramatic events (lightning, tornadoes)
are D-state traversals — transient passages through the dark
state where both sides are momentarily in the gap.

## Lightning

**The stick:** insulating atmosphere. Charge builds.
**The slip:** dielectric breakdown. Current flows.
**The D-state:** the ionized channel (neither insulating nor
conducting — both states in the gap simultaneously).

### Predictions

- Branch pattern follows the Stern-Brocot tree
- Fractal dimension: 0.855 × embedding dimension
  - 2D projection: 0.855 × 2 = 1.71 (observed: 1.7 ± 0.1) ✓
  - 3D: 0.855 × 3 = 2.57
- Return stroke (the bright part) = the D-state traversal
- Probability of strike ∝ (field - threshold)² (Born rule)

### Testable with existing data

Lichtenberg figure databases, lightning mapping arrays (LMA),
and high-speed camera footage of lightning branching.

## Tornadoes

**The stick:** laminar or weakly rotating flow.
**The slip:** vortex locks to a specific rotation rate.
**The D-state:** the mesocyclone-to-tornado transition
(the rotation intensifies from diffuse to concentrated).

### Predictions

- Formation probability P ∝ (K - K_c)² where K is wind shear
  and K_c is the threshold for vortex locking (Born rule)
- EF scale maps to tongue denominator classes
- Formation time τ ∝ 1/√(K - K_c) (critical slowing)
- Only ~25% of mesocyclones produce tornadoes (the fraction
  of the parameter distribution that falls inside the tongue)

### Testable with existing data

SPC (Storm Prediction Center) probabilistic forecasts vs
observed tornado occurrence. The relationship between
environmental parameters (CAPE, shear, SRH) and tornado
probability should follow the parabolic onset P ∝ ε².

## Earthquakes

The most literal stick-slip system in nature.

**The stick:** locked fault. Stress builds.
**The slip:** rupture. Seismic waves propagate.
**The D-state:** the rupture front (the moving boundary
between locked and slipping fault segments).

### Predictions

- The Gutenberg-Richter law (log N ∝ -bM) is the Farey
  measure applied to fault slip amplitudes
- The b-value (typically ~1) should relate to the Stern-Brocot
  tree dimension (0.855 × embedding)
- Aftershock sequences should follow the devil's staircase
  (Omori's law as a staircase in time)

## Status

All three are **concept-level**. The lightning fractal dimension
(0.855 × 2 = 1.71) is the most quantitative prediction and
matches literature. The tornado Born rule and earthquake
Gutenberg-Richter connections need domain-specific computation.
